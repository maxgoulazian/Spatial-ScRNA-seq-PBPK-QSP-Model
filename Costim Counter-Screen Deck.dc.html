02 · The Problem → The Instrument
Costim engagers · counter-screen logic

A costim arm can't avoid CD4, and costim amplifies CD4's toxicity. So don't build a screen — build a counter-screen.

THE PROBLEM

Anti-CD3 sits on every T cell, so the molecule can't avoid CD4⁺ — and costimulation amplifies whatever CD4 does.

The enemy is a CD4 sub-program — the Treg-suppressive, high-cytokine wiring — not the CD4 lineage.

CRS  CD4 helpers drive IFN-γ / TNF / IL-2 → myeloid IL-6.
TREG  Tregs are CD4⁺, CD28/4-1BB-high — costim expands the brakes.
PRECEDENT  TGN1412 → near-fatal CRS; 4-1BB agonists → hepatotoxicity.
LIABILITY — WANT LOW

CRSTreg-suppressionhelp-erosionexhaustionproliferation
BENEFIT — WANT HIGH
CD8⁺ CRISPRa effector
Schmidt et al. 2022 · IFN-γ · gain-of-function
The killing is done by CD8. The toxicity is written in CD4. Score them on separate axes.
CAVEAT — CRISPRi is loss-of-function; an engager arm is gain-of-function. Agonism = −1 × knockdown is a mechanistic prior, corroborated by an independent gain-of-function ORF screen.
