# PART I — SUBMISSION NARRATIVE

## A. Executive summary

CD3 T-cell engagers deliver activation signal 1 but no costimulatory signal 2; the field adds a costim arm (CD28, 4-1BB), but because the CD3 arm engages **every** T cell it also engages CD4⁺ cells, and costimulation then amplifies the CD4 suppressive (Treg/IL-10) and cytokine-release (CRS) programs that drive toxicity. We used a **genome-scale CD4⁺ CRISPRi Perturb-seq map (Zhu et al. 2025)** as a *counter-screen* — the one compartment where effector/help wiring can be separated from suppressive/CRS wiring — and scored a 12-arm costim panel on effector benefit, six CD4 liability axes with a liability **veto gate**, and CD8-vs-CD4 selectivity.

**The finding:** exactly **two of twelve** arms pass the liability veto clean — **4-1BB (TNFRSF9) and CD27** — and both are independently CD8-selective by *complementary* measures (4-1BB by effector-network wiring ~19×; CD27 by receptor expression, CD8−CD4 +0.46). The arm with the **highest** raw effector signal, CD28 (Schmidt z = 12.11), is **gated out** on three liabilities — the clearest proof that the counter-screen, not effector strength, drives the nomination. The nomination survived an unbiased genome-wide effector-first start and a bidirectional GOF/LOF concordance check (joint P(top-2) rises 0.528 → 0.836 when weighted by expression).

**Buildability:** de novo agonist binders were designed against the co-leads with RFdiffusion/RFantibody (backbones complete for 4-1BB, CD27, and the CD3 arm), with a 4-1BB epitope validated as clustering-compatible (non-blocking to ligand/multimer — the structural prerequisite for allosteric agonism).

## B. Framing — the two-part problem

**One-line thesis.** Costim-engager toxicity is *two* problems — a **wiring** problem (which costim program a receptor feeds) and a **where/when** problem (whether signal 2 fires only where signal 1 does). A CD4⁺ single-cell counter-screen solves the first; a per-cell spatial QSP model solves the second. The "wrong subset" screen turns out to be the right instrument for the wiring half.

---

## Why reframe

The original framing is **accurate and should be kept at its core**, but it is a *single-lever* framing (pick a clean receptor) describing a *two-lever* result (clean receptor **+** coincidence-gated format). The strongest things we found — active CD8-selectivity and the cis/trans format axis — have no home in the single-lever version.

### Original opening (for reference)
> CD3 engagers deliver signal 1 but no signal 2 — so the field adds a costimulatory arm. The unsolved problem is toxicity. Because the engaging arm is anti-CD3 — present on every T cell — the molecule cannot avoid CD4⁺, and costimulation amplifies whatever CD4⁺ does. The enemy is a CD4 sub-program — the Treg-suppressive, high-cytokine wiring — not the CD4 lineage. CD4 help is beneficial.

### What is load-bearing and stays (verbatim)
- **"The enemy is a sub-program, not a lineage."** The crown jewel — counterintuitive, correct, differentiating. The nomination separates effector wiring from suppressive/CRS wiring *within* CD4, and it held (4-1BB + CD27 clean on all six liability axes, effector-beneficial, survived the unbiased genome-wide start).
- **"CD4 help is beneficial."** Separates us from the naive Treg-depletion crowd.
- **Toxicity as the unsolved problem** (TGN1412, urelumab hepatotox).

## What the single-lever framing undersells

1. **Our positive criterion is stronger than "clean."** The sharpest results are *active CD8-selectivity*, not just liability-cleanliness:
   - **CD27** leads on receptor **expression** selectivity (CD8−CD4 ≈ +0.46; 4-1BB ≈ +0.04; CD28 negative).
   - **4-1BB** leads on effector-**network** selectivity (~19× CD8-vs-CD4 drive; net +54).
   - → The co-leads are CD8-biased by **complementary** measures (CD27 by *where expressed*, 4-1BB by *how wired*). "The boost lands on the killers" beats "the boost doesn't hit the brakes."

2. **Toxicity is multi-origin, and only one origin is receptor-soluble.** Of the three mechanisms, only **Treg expansion** is truly a CD4-subset problem. **CRS breadth** is magnitude-driven / myeloid-amplified; **costim-intrinsic toxicity** (TGN1412, 4-1BB hepatotox) is subset-independent. So "the enemy is a CD4 sub-program" is honestly ~⅓ of the toxicity. The other ⅔ is a *where/when/how-much* problem — magnitude control, coincidence-gating, tumor-conditional delivery — which is exactly the **cis/trans QSP axis**. In the single-lever framing that work is a homeless bolt-on.

---

## The reframe: two-part nomination

**Part 1 — the receptor.** Sub-program-clean **and** CD8-selective → **4-1BB / CD27**. Scored by the CD4 Perturb-seq counter-screen (6 liability axes + effector benefit + expression/network selectivity).

**Part 2 — the format.** Deliver it coincidence-gated so signal 2 fires only where signal 1 does → **cis geometry / tumor-conditional**. Scored by the per-cell spatial QSP model (cis/trans span sweep, Treg-axis differential).

**Unifying line.** *Costim-engager toxicity is a wiring problem and a where/when problem; single-cell data solves the first, the QSP model solves the second, and the "wrong subset" screen turns out to be the right instrument for the wiring half.*

### Three evidence pillars
| Pillar | Claim | Primary instrument |
|---|---|---|
| **Wiring (receptor)** | 4-1BB/CD27 are sub-program-clean + CD8-selective | CD4 Perturb-seq counter-screen; Schmidt CD8 effector anchor |
| **Where/when (format)** | Cis (height-matched) gating narrows the therapeutic window vs trans | Per-cell spatial QSP, cis/trans span sweep |
| **Buildability** | Both co-leads are structurally druggable at agonist-compatible epitopes | RFdiffusion/RFantibody de novo binders + clustering-compatibility check |

## Positioning notes
- **Supporting, not headline:** the redirector×costim co-expression axis (CD3 vs alternative redirectors) is a *third* selectivity layer — keep as a supporting result, it's the least mature of the three.
- **Instrument-forward variant** ("the wrong-subset screen is the right instrument") is elegant but abstract — use as a mid-talk reveal, not the cold open. Lead with the therapeutic payoff.
- **Honest scope carried forward:** CRISPRi is loss-of-function while an engager arm is gain-of-function (no screen proves agonism helps — QSP translates the validated state change); CD4 is the counter-screen, not the effector axis; cis epitope is hypothetical (no designed binder yet), CRD1 is the one with an actual design.

*This framing is tighter and more novel than the original, not looser: it is honest about multi-origin toxicity, promotes the QSP cis/trans work to a co-equal pillar, and keeps the counterintuitive core intact.*

## C. Why this is novel

- **The 'wrong subset' screen is the right instrument.** Nobody had mined a CD4 Perturb-seq map to *separate* costim effector wiring from the suppressive/CRS wiring that gates engager toxicity. The counter-screen reframes CD4 from liability to instrument.
- **Active CD8-selectivity, not just cleanliness.** The co-leads are CD8-biased by two orthogonal measures (network + expression), so the costim boost lands on killers while sparing the CD4/Treg/CRS axis.
- **A genome-wide CRS master-regulator scan** surfaced **87 storm safety-valves** (a novel target *class*, led by UVSSA), not just another costim receptor — the first mining of this screen for CRS suppressors in the engager context.
- **End-to-end in one week, expert-directed with Claude as the multiplier** across literature review, target screening, data digitization, GRN inference, per-cell spatial QSP, verification, and de novo protein design — work that normally takes a multidisciplinary team months.

## D. Honest scope (carried into the submission)

- **CRISPRi is loss-of-function; an engager arm is gain-of-function.** No screen can prove agonism helps — the screen supplies a validated state change; the QSP model translates it into predicted efficacy and window.
- **CD4 is the counter-screen, not the effector axis.** CD8 does the killing (Schmidt CD8 anchor); we do not claim to measure CD8 killing in CD4 data.
- **Toxicity is multi-origin.** Only Treg expansion is a true CD4-subset problem; CRS breadth (magnitude/myeloid-amplified) and costim-intrinsic toxicity (TGN1412, 4-1BB hepatotox) are subset-independent and are addressed by the *format* pillar.
- **The cis (height-matched) epitope is hypothetical** — CRD1 is the one with an actual designed binder; the cis/trans choice is a QSP-adjudicable format parameter, not a settled result.
- **QSP results are preliminary** — the per-cell spatial model is in active development; format-pillar numbers are excluded from this audit pass and captured in the companion QSP audit (§11).

## Track 2 — Genome-wide GRN Inference (hero CD4 Perturb-seq → drive operator)

### What was done (step by step)

**1. 1. Substrate assembly — pseudobulk → per-perturbation collapse** — Starts from GWCD4i.pseudobulk_merged.h5ad (278,684 pseudobulk guide×donor×condition profiles × 18,129 genes, raw summed UMI, library sizes 6.0k–5.2M). Restricts to the Stim48hr sustained-activation state and keep_for_DE=True QC-pass profiles (78,280 profiles), CP10K+log1p normalizes, then collapses by n_cells-weighted mean per perturbed_gene to one expression profile per CRISPRi target → 11,332 perturbation profiles × 18,127 genes (dropped the non-ENSG spike-in feature + 1 zero-variance gene). Genes handled in Ensembl-ID space.  
*Why:* The activated Stim48hr state is where costim wiring and the CRS/suppressive programs are engaged; perturbation-driven variance across knockdowns is the exact signal GRN inference exploits to reconstruct regulatory wiring.  
*Inputs:* GWCD4i.pseudobulk_merged.h5ad (hero_cd4_perturbseq_zhu2025)  
*Tools/methods:* scanpy/anndata; CP10K + log1p; n_cells-weighted mean collapse per perturbed_gene; Ensembl-ID gene space  
*Outputs:* expr matrix 11,332 perturbation profiles × 18,127 genes (GRNBoost2 substrate; intermediate)  
*Key stats:* 278,684 pseudobulk → 78,280 Stim48hr QC-pass profiles → 11,332 per-perturbation profiles × 18,127 genes; raw UMI verified integer, library sizes 6.0k–5.2M

**2. 2. Regulator set assembly (1,598 regulators)** — Builds the regulator (feature) set for GRN inference: 1,569 canonical human TFs from Lambert_2018_HumanTF.csv (is_TF=Yes) intersected with the matrix, UNION the 12 costim receptors (TNFRSF9, CD27, TNFRSF4, TNFRSF18, TNFRSF25, TNFRSF14, TNFRSF8, CD28, ICOS, CD226, CD2, CD40) and the program genes (CRS ∪ SUPP ∪ HELP). Targets = all 18,127 genes (genome-scale).  
*Why:* Adding the 12 costim receptors and program genes as regulators guarantees the costim→program subnetwork is complete and directly queryable for the toxicity/effector drive computation.  
*Inputs:* Lambert_2018_HumanTF.csv (project reference dir); 12 costim receptor list; program gene sets  
*Tools/methods:* set intersection/union in Ensembl-ID space  
*Outputs:* regulator list = 1,598 regulators (1,569 TFs + costim + program genes)  
*Key stats:* 1,569 TFs (Lambert 2018) → UNION costim+program → 1,598 total regulators; 18,127 targets

**3. 3. GRNBoost2 genome-scale inference** — Infers a weighted directed regulatory network with arboreto GRNBoost2 (gradient-boosting core of SCENIC): per-target stochastic gradient-boosting regression (learning_rate 0.01, n_estimators 5000, max_features 0.1, subsample 0.9, early-stopping window 25); feature importances = directed regulator→target edge weights. Because arboreto 0.1.6's Dask-graph assembly is incompatible with Dask 2026.7, the SAME arboreto.core.infer_partial_network regressor is driven per target across a 20-process pool (seed 777); targets ordered regulators+program-genes first so the costim→program subnetwork completes under any timebox.  
*Why:* Produces the genome-scale regulatory map that the toxicity/effector program-drive is propagated over — the mechanistic backbone of the QSP drive operator.  
*Inputs:* expr 11,332×18,127 substrate (step 1); 1,598 regulators (step 2)  
*Tools/methods:* arboreto GRNBoost2 (SGBM: lr 0.01, n_estimators 5000, max_features 0.1, subsample 0.9, early-stop window 25); infer_partial_network per-target across 20-process pool; seed 777; env 'grn'; driver run_grn_pool.py  
*Outputs:* grn_edges_raw.parquet (raw unsigned GRNBoost2 importances; intermediate → cd4_grn_edges_full.parquet)  
*Key stats:* 1,653,594 edges, 1,598 regulators × 18,127 targets; unsigned importance range 6.38e-15 to 44.671

**4. 4. Edge signing (Pearson correlation)** — GRNBoost2 importances are unsigned. Each edge is signed by the Pearson correlation of regulator and target across the 11,332 profiles (CellOracle convention): signed_importance = sign(pearson_r) × importance, giving directional edges for propagation.  
*Why:* Directionality (activating vs repressing) is required to propagate an agonism (+Δ) shift correctly onto each program and to compute signed drive.  
*Inputs:* grn_edges_raw.parquet; expr substrate  
*Tools/methods:* Pearson r(regulator,target) over 11,332 profiles; signed_importance = sign(r)·importance; annotated by analyze_grn.py (env 'grn')  
*Outputs:* cd4_grn_edges_full.parquet (signed); cd4_grn_edges_TFtarget_full.csv (GRNBoost2-native TF,target,importance,validated)  
*Key stats:* pearson_r range [-0.781, 0.944]; signed_importance range [-22.934, 44.671]; 40.8% negative (repressing) / 59.2% positive (activating) edges

**5. 5. CRISPRi validation against DE gold standard** — Validates inferred edges against each receptor's own CRISPRi knockdown signature in the independent DE matrix (GWCD4i.DE_stats.h5ad, layers['log_fc'], Stim48hr). Three per-receptor metrics: (1) Magnitude — fraction of DE-testable out-edges whose target is significantly moved (adj_p<0.1); (2) Enrichment — |log_fc| of edge-targets vs non-edge genes (one-sided Mann–Whitney, rank-biserial AUC); (3) Direction — does edge sign predict KD direction (sign(log_fc)==−edge_sign among moved targets, one-sided binomial). Plus network-wide importance→magnitude and top-25 enrichment cross-checks.  
*Why:* Turns a co-expression GRN into a Perturb-seq-validated network: the top-K enrichment and direction concordance are the evidence that GRN edges reflect real causal knockdown effects, licensing the propagation-based drive.  
*Inputs:* cd4_grn_edges_full.parquet; reg_DE_stim48.npz (from GWCD4i.DE_stats.h5ad log_fc, Stim48hr)  
*Tools/methods:* Mann–Whitney U (one-sided), binomial test, Pearson within-TF; adj_p<0.1 strict threshold; analyze_grn.py  
*Outputs:* validated + de_testable + dir_concordant columns on cd4_grn_edges_full.parquet  
*Key stats:* de_testable 886,237 (53.6%); validated (adj_p<0.1) 8,457 = 0.5114% of all edges / 0.954% of testable; dir_concordant on 8,457 moved edges = 65.5% concordance. Methods headline metrics: top-25 edge enrichment mean AUC 0.583 (69.8% AUC>0.5, p=1.7e-16); importance→KD magnitude mean within-TF r=+0.097 (78.7% positive, p=7.6e-10); edge-sign→KD direction 61.7% concordance (p=3.3e-198); all-edge enrichment AUC 0.547

**6. 6. Costim → program DRIVE (network propagation) + LOF→GOF flip** — For each costim receptor, computes downstream drive onto four programs by signed network propagation from the receptor node through a bounded multi-hop kernel K = I + α·T + α²·T² (α=0.5) over the regulator subspace, then a final row-normalized regulator→gene hop. The CRISPRi screen is loss-of-function; an engager arm is gain-of-function, so the drive is computed as a +Δ (agonism) shift on the costim node (agonism = −1×knockdown-direction). All reported drive/z are on the agonism axis (positive = agonism raises that program). This is the signed-linear-propagation proxy for CellOracle's simulate_shift (CellOracle unavailable in env).  
*Why:* This is the operation that turns the GRN into a QSP drive: it maps each candidate costim arm to how much its agonism raises/lowers effector, CRS, suppression, and help — the efficacy and toxicity terms the QSP model consumes.  
*Inputs:* cd4_grn_edges_full.parquet (signed); program_gene_sets.json  
*Tools/methods:* signed linear network propagation, kernel K=I+αT+α²T² (α=0.5), row-normalized signed weights; agonism sign flip; CellOracle-proxy  
*Outputs:* cd4_costim_program_drive.csv (raw drive; intermediate); → cd4_grn_qsp_drive_full.csv  
*Key stats:* Program sets: effector=IFNG,GZMB,PRF1,TNF,IL2,TBX21,EOMES,NKG7,FASLG,KLRG1; CRS=TNF,IL2,IFNG; SUPP=15 genes (CTLA4,FOXP3,IL10,IL2RA,TIGIT,LAG3,TNFRSF18,...); HELP=BCL6,MAF,TOX2,CXCR5,TCF7. 43 receptor input-node instances mapped, 16 unique signaling nodes

**7. 7. *Why:* Puts the four program drives on a common, interpretable scale (0 = pan-T backbone) so the QSP model can read costim-arm efficacy/toxicity directly.  
*Inputs:* cd4_costim_program_drive.csv; 600-random-regulator null

*Outputs:* cd4_grn_qsp_drive_full.csv  
*Key stats:* 12 receptors × 4 programs. Primary (empirical-null) z. Raw-drive ranges: effector[-1.549,5.832], crs[-0.842,1.626], supp[-0.786,7.259], help[-0.177,8.853]. Max suppression liability TNFRSF9 & TNFRSF4 (supp_z +2.64); CD28 supp_z +2.36 (pan-costim ref); TNFRSF8/CD30 lowest toxicity (supp −1.65, crs −2.24)

**1. S1 — Epitope selection & agonism-target definition (per receptor)** — For each of the 4 arms, defines the target epitope + hotspot residues that RFdiffusion is conditioned on, and the MECHANISTIC agonism rationale that dictates WHICH epitope. Two costim receptors get opposite strategies: 4-1BB (TNFRSF9, UniProt Q07011) = NON-ligand-blocking CRD1 / urelumab-class epitope (membrane-distal), so 4-1BBL trimerization/clustering is preserved AND augmented by binder crosslinking -> superagonism; hotspot_res=[25,26,27,28,30,31,32,35,39,40,41,42,43,44,45] on template 6MHR chain C; AVOID_res=[49..103] = the 4-1BBL CRD2-3 clustering interface (utomilumab mode=weak). *Why:* The whole nomination rests on agonism-direction: a costim arm must AGONIZE (cluster/crosslink) its receptor, not merely bind. **2. S2 — 4-1BB clustering-compatibility agonist filter (built + validated on controls)** — A geometric discriminator that scores whether a designed 4-1BB binder permits (vs blocks) receptor clustering. Method: superimpose the designed binder onto each of the 3 receptor copies in the 6MGP 4-1BBL:4-1BB 3:3 assembly (Kabsch on shared receptor CA), then score (1) heavy-atom clash vs the 4-1BBL trimer, (2) inter-copy binder-binder clash, (3) radial placement vs receptor centroid. PASS = ligand_clash_atoms==0 AND inter_binder_clash==0.  
*Why:* This is the agonism gate that a loss-of-function CRISPRi screen structurally cannot supply: it operationalizes 'will this binder superagonize 4-1BB?' as a reproducible structural test, distinguishing urelumab-mode (clustering-compatible superagonist) from utomilumab-mode (ligand-blocking weak agonist). It is the STAGE-D filter that turns 'binds confidently' (AF3) into 'will agonize' (6MGP).  
*Inputs:* PDB 6MGP 3:3 assembly (6MGP-assembly1.cif); Urelumab pose (from 6MHR); Utomilumab pose (from 6MI2)  
*Tools/methods:* Kabsch superposition on shared receptor CA; heavy-atom clash counting; 4-1BB-specific (CD27 uses ligand-mimicry+multivalency, CD3 uses epitope-fidelity, CEA5 has no agonist filter).

## 9. CONSOLIDATED FLAGS — prioritized (the 'nothing outdated / nothing wrong' check)

*Grouped by severity. None of these change the co-lead nomination (it is a drive-independent liability gate), but each needs handling before figures/claims go into the deck.*

### A. Presentation-critical (fix wording or a reader will be misled)

1. **QSP columns are in the master table but NOT audited/final.** `qsp_window`, `qsp_effector_z`, `TI`, A31/A31b window numbers are present and quoted in the markdown docs, but the QSP model is in active development — these were **not** re-verified. The nomination does **not** depend on them (gate is drive-independent). → In the deck, either label QSP numbers 'preliminary (model in development)' or omit until the cis/trans curves land.
2. **'6-axis liability veto' is really 5 gating axes.** 6 axes are *scored* (CRS, SUPP, HELP, PROLIF, EXH, DD_SUPP) but DD_SUPP adds **zero** independent gate calls. → Say '6 axes scored, 5 gate' to stay precise.
3. **Three distinct 'Schmidt effector z' quantities exist and are NOT interchangeable** — E_schmidt_z (primary axis, used in master table), A30 effector_gof_z (Calabrese CRISPRa GOF, what the A30 Monte Carlo samples), and A0diff_CD8_IFNG_z (CD8-vs-CD4 differential). For 4-1BB they nearly coincide (3.741 vs 3.754); for CD27 they diverge (4.278 vs 3.745). CD28 shows as 12.11 (E_schmidt) vs 11.94 (A0diff CD8) vs 10.73 (A30 GOF). → Any figure caption must name *which* effector z it plots.
4. **GRN validation headline must be the top-K metric, not 0.51%.** The 'validated' boolean (adj_p<0.1) is 0.51% of edges — a conservative floor deflated by 10k-gene multiple testing. The real evidence is **top-25 enrichment AUC 0.583 + 61.7% sign-direction concordance**. → Present the top-K metric.
5. **GRN in-silico perturbation is a signed-linear-propagation proxy, NOT a trained CellOracle sim** (CellOracle not installable in-env; disclosed in methods §7). → Keep the disclosure in any GRN figure caption.
